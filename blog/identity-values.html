Identity, Values, and Strengths

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I am still pondering this one.


Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

New Zealand has a fairly individualistic culture, and while I was growing up I definitely learned to rely solely on myself to solve problems and get things done. This has made me capable in a lot of areas but also stunted my ability to ask for help when needed.

I value honesty and truth but I haven't always been honest with myself and others about when I was overwhelmed and needed help on a project. One of my old managers understood this about me and tried to coach me towards better communication practices. I am reminded of him whenever I recognise myself falling back into bad communication habits.

My hope is that ultimately I can overcome that failing and become a good communicator of not just the technical side of things, but also the human side of things too.


Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey

I believe that I am a good listener. When someone is talking, I hear them the whole way through, try to understand their point of view, and ask clarifying questions if I don't understand them at first. This helps when working in teams as I always approach teamwork in a fair and democratic way, which people seem to appreciate.

I am open minded to new ideas and different ways of doing things, and I have a genuine thirst for knowledge. This means that I always have a good time learning something new, as long as I have an interest in the topic.

I can read and write fairly well, so I can generally figure out what is being asked of me without trouble. In team activities I am often the scribe for the group. There are many people who don't like the documentation side of projects, so in the past I have fulfilled this role.


Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

In the past I have sometimes struggled to effectively communicate when I am struggling with a project and need help. I need to actively acknowledge to myself when I feel that way, and seek help when I need it.

If I feel that a task is boring, repetitive, and not new in any way, I often seek out distractions to entertain myself. This can take up too much valuable time, so I need to be aware of when I am getting bored, acknowledge it, and maybe take a timed break so that I can be productive again.


Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

At an old job there was someone who constantly spoke in a very negative tone about their work, clients, and life in general. It was negatively affecting the rest of their team, to the point where it became very difficult to focus while working near them.

The person was my manager, so I found it difficult to address the issue directly with them. I broached the topic with their manager, who said it would be best if I addressed the problem directly with my manager. It was daunting but I asked my manager to sit with me one-on-one and they agreed.

When I explained to them the effect that their behaviour was having on the people around them, they apologised and let me know that they had been going through rough times, which I sympathised with. We were able to work through it, and although their behaviour was never perfect, it did improve a lot, which made it much easier to work with them.

This helped me learn the power of facing issues head-on, but also being understanding, patient, and graceful with the people you work with, even when they're making things harder for everyone else. We're all human after all.

If I had to do it again, I think I would do it the same way, but perhaps a lot earlier on. I really doubted my ability to resolve the situation at the time but I don't anymore.
